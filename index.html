About the Virtual Monster

The T206 superset — known to collectors as "the Monster" — is widely regarded as the most complex checklist in the hobby. Issued by the American Tobacco Company between 1909 and 1911 across more than a dozen cigarette brands, the full universe of front-and-back combinations numbers in the thousands. Significant work has been done toward documenting it — most notably Bill Brown's widely respected superset checklist and the scholarship at T206Resource.com — but the set's sheer scope means the record remains a living document. This archive builds on that foundation, combining graded certification as its evidentiary backbone with community expertise and collective knowledge as the connective tissue.

The Virtual Monster was conceived as a community effort to extend that work: a validated, crowd-sourced database where every documented combination is anchored to a real, graded card — not a rumor, not a memory, not a scan of uncertain provenance. Grading certification numbers serve as the authentication mechanism, cross-referenceable against public registries maintained by PSA and SGC. The checklist itself is anchored to Bill Brown's T206 Super Set Checklist — 6,465 documented front-and-back combinations across 36 backs — and every combination in this archive maps directly to that reference.

How It Works

Each entry in the checklist represents a unique front/back combination. When a collector submits a graded card, the cert number is cross-referenced against PSA or SGC's public registry and linked to its combination. The highest-graded verified example is featured as the Finest Known — a living title that updates automatically when a superior example is documented. Submissions are credited to the contributor who brought the example to the record, independent of current ownership.

Submission Policies

  • PSA certifications are verified automatically at submission against the PSA public registry
  • SGC certifications are accepted pending brief administrative review against the SGC registry
  • BGS, CSG, and other third-party graders are accepted pending administrative review
  • Raw (ungraded) cards are accepted with front and back scans at 300 DPI minimum
  • Raw submissions are catalogued in the Attested tier — above Reported, below Confirmed — and do not compete with graded examples for Finest Known status; only a submitted cert number elevates a combination to Confirmed
  • Submitters are credited by their chosen handle for bringing the example to the record; credit reflects documentation, not ownership
  • If a cert has already been confirmed in the archive, subsequent submissions of the same cert number are blocked as duplicates
  • When two submissions carry equal grades, PSA and SGC are ranked above other graders; at equal grade and grader, the earlier submission date holds Finest Known — first to document is first in the record
  • Qualified grades (OC, MC, ST, MK, PD, etc.) are ranked using the PSA Registry deduction scale: grades 4–9 with any qualifier are treated as two points lower for Finest Known purposes; grades 2–3 lose one point; grades 1 and 1.5 are unaffected — a PSA 8(OC) ranks as a PSA 6
  • Suspected forgeries or authentication concerns are flagged and reviewed by the administrative panel

Checklist Philosophy

  • A combination is marked Confirmed when at least one graded submission with a verifiable cert exists
  • A combination is marked Attested when submitted with front and back scans but no grading cert — community members may upvote to signal confidence or downvote to flag doubts; a net score of −3 or below flags the submission for administrative review
  • A combination is marked Reported when present in the Bill Brown checklist but not yet submitted to this archive
  • Attested is a permanent status until a graded cert is submitted for the same combination — community voting signals confidence and can queue a combination for priority graded verification, but scans alone do not upgrade a card to Confirmed
  • Combinations outside the Bill Brown checklist are not assumed to exist by default; they may be submitted through the Attested pathway and, if confirmed with a valid PSA or SGC cert, are added to the archive immediately
  • New combination discoveries submitted with BGS, CSG, or other third-party certs are accepted pending administrative review before Confirmed status is granted
  • Back variations (factory numbers, series text) are treated as distinct combinations
  • The Finest Known title is held by the single highest-graded confirmed submission per combination; it updates automatically whenever a superior example is documented

Curatorial Decisions

The Virtual Monster's checklist is anchored to Bill Brown's T206 Super Set Checklist, the most widely respected community reference document. Several edge cases required explicit decisions:

  • Ty Cobb back — excluded. The Ty Cobb cigarette brand back appears on exactly one card (Cobb's red portrait) and is widely regarded as a novelty rather than a standard advertising back. Because it does not appear in Bill Brown's checklist, it is excluded here for consistency. Collectors who wish to document a Ty Cobb-backed Cobb may note it in their submission's comments field.
  • Blank backs — treated as printing errors, not a back variety. Cards with no back printing are production defects — unintended byproducts of the manufacturing process rather than a deliberate advertising configuration. They are not catalogued as a distinct back in this archive. Blank-backed cards may be submitted and will be noted in the combination record, but they do not generate a separate checklist entry.
  • Old Mill Southern League Brown ink — treated as a printing anomaly, not a separate back. The overwhelming majority of Old Mill Southern League backs are printed in black ink. The rare brown-ink variant is generally considered by advanced collectors and major auction houses to be a press artifact rather than an intentional back variation. Cards bearing this characteristic may be submitted and will be flagged as a brown-ink variant within the Old Mill Southern League entry, but they do not constitute a separate combination for Finest Known purposes.

These decisions are not immutable. If community consensus shifts — or if compelling new scholarship emerges — the curatorial stance can be revisited. The goal is a stable, defensible baseline, not the last word.

A Note on Completeness

The checklist is, by its nature, never finished. New combinations surface every year. This archive does not claim to be the final word — it claims only to be the most rigorously documented word available at any given moment. If you hold a card that isn't in the checklist, that is reason to submit it, not reason to doubt it.
